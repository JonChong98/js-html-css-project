My Secondary Education: Bukit Batok Secondary School

I was a student at Bukit Batok Secondary School (BBSS) from the year 2011-2014. My PSLE results allowed me to enter the express stream and from Secondary 1-2, I studied the same base subjects as my fellow peers: English, Mother Tongue, Science (Physics/ Chemistry), Pure Humanities (Geography/History), Literature, Art, Home Economics and Design and Technology. Along with these base subjects, I took on Malay as my third Mother Tongue under the Malay Special Program (MSP) from Secondary 1 till Secondary 4. I performed well in my first 2 years in BBSS and was given the choice to enter either the Arts or Science stream, along with which subjects I wanted to study in Secondary 3 and 4 when it came time for subject streaming at the end of Secondary 2.

I entered the Science Stream in Secondary 3 and 4 and took on 9 subjects: English, Mother Tongue (Chinese), MSP (Malay), Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Additional Math, Elementary Math, Combined Humanities (Elective Geography and Social Studies). I chose to study 3 Pure Sciences in Secondary 3 and 4 because of my interest in Science, despite being told that it would be a challenge due to how content heavy it would be. Due to the updated facilities of my Secondary School, I was able to perform many practical science experiments that I never had the opportunity to do so before, which further nurtured my interest in Science and allowed me to excel. I performed well throughout my last 2 years in BBSS and was able to attain an ‘O’-level score of 10.

While I was in BBSS, I joined BB as my CCA due to my interest in the Uniformed Group. I participated actively in my CCA throughout my 4 years in BBSS, going for numerous competitions such as a drill competition and the ‘CharacterQuest’ competitions. I also participated in 2 overseas CIP trips to Cambodia, where a team of students and teachers from the Boys’ Brigade and Girls’ Brigade companies in BBSS flew over and carried out various projects such as refurbishing a library in an orphanage and teaching English to the local students.


In my time studying at BBSS, I learnt valuable lessons in leadership through my experiences in my CCA: Boys’ Brigade and was able to nurture my interest in Science with the help of the many teachers who taught me. This eventually led me to discover my interest in Computer Science and the decision to pursue my current course in Singapore Polytechnic now.
